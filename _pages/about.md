I am a Research Scientist at the [Transportation Research Center Inc.](https://www.trcpg.com/), where I work on vehicle safety research focused on Automated Driving Systems (ADS) and Advanced Driver Assistance Systems (ADAS). My work spans the full lifecycle of ADS validation: deploying and tuning open-source autonomy stacks ([Autoware.ai](https://github.com/autowarefoundation/autoware_ai)/Universe) on Level 4 experimental platforms, building large-scale data pipelines for crash and naturalistic-driving analysis, and developing statistically rigorous frameworks for rare-event risk estimation.

I received my Ph.D. in Electrical and Computer Engineering from [The Ohio State University](https://www.osu.edu/) in 2023, advised by [Prof. Keith Redmill](http://www2.ece.ohio-state.edu/~redmill/) and [Prof. Umit Ozguner](https://citr.osu.edu/CrIS/Umit_Ozguner.html) at the [Control and Intelligent Transportation Lab](https://citr.osu.edu/). My dissertation, *"Model-based Falsification and Safety Evaluation of Autonomous Systems,"* developed adversarial-scenario generation and dynamic probabilistic risk assessment methods for ADS testing.

Previously I interned at [Motional](https://motional.com/), [Oztech Inc.](https://www.oztinc.com/), and [Takeda Lab](https://takedalab.g.sp.m.is.nagoya-u.ac.jp/home) at [Nagoya University](https://en.nagoya-u.ac.jp/). I earned my Bachelor's in Electronics and Control from [Escuela Politécnica Nacional](https://www.epn.edu.ec/) in Quito, Ecuador, graduating *Summa Cum Laude* and as the best graduate of the 2015 class.

I am a [Fulbright Scholar](https://eca.state.gov/fulbright/about-fulbright/fulbright-program-overview) (2017 to 2019) and was named a 2023 [OSU Presidential Fellow](https://gradsch.osu.edu/presidential-fellowship), the Graduate School's most prestigious award.
